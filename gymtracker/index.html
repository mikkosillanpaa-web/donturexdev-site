About GymTracker

GymTracker helps you plan your strength training, log what you do in each session and stay on top of progression. You define your workout days and exercise library, the app takes care of sets in the session, rest timers and coach suggestions based on your recent performance.

Program

Workout days & exercise library

  • Create multiple workout days (e.g. Day 1: Upper, Day 2: Lower).
  • Add exercises from your own library with muscle group, type, default sets, reps and weight.
  • Reorder and edit movements per day without touching your history.
  • Tap on an exercise name to open its long‑term progress view (estimated 1RM chart).
During workout

Warm-up, sets and rest timer

  • Optional general warm‑up card at the start of the session – choose activity and track time.
  • Session screen shows all movements with planned sets and rep‑ranges.
  • Tap a set to mark it done; long‑press to adjust planned weight and reps.
  • Built‑in rest timer starts automatically after a working set, shows remaining time in the top bar and via a notification, with configurable vibration.
Coach

Progression, variation & deload

  • Progression coach suggests when to increase, maintain or decrease weight based on recent sessions.
  • Variation suggestions when a lift stalls: slightly lighter weight and an extra set for 1–2 sessions.
  • Deload detection at muscle‑group level when signs of fatigue accumulate – lighter weight and sometimes fewer sets without touching your base settings.
  • Modes for both progression and deload: suggestions only or auto‑apply with an info dialog listing changes at session start.

How GymTracker works

GymTracker is built around three main flows: planning your program, logging sessions and reviewing your history with coach suggestions.

1. Set up your program

  • Open Settings → Workout days to define your training split (e.g. 3‑day push/pull/legs).
  • Add exercises to each day from the Exercises library.
  • Each exercise can have default sets, reps and starting weight; you can fine‑tune per session.

2. Log your workouts

  • From the main Workout tab or a day detail screen, tap Start workout.
  • Use the set chips to mark sets as done; GymTracker tracks reps and weight for each set.
  • The rest timer and warm‑up entries are saved to the session note, visible later in the history.

3. Use history & coach

  • The History tab shows all completed workouts with date and duration.
  • Tapping a workout opens its details: per‑exercise set breakdown and warm‑up notes.
  • The coach uses this history to build suggestions for future sessions: progression, variation or deload.

Coach modes & visibility

Coach settings are under Settings → Coach. There you can:

  • Enable/disable weight progression and choose between suggestions only or auto‑apply.
  • Enable/disable deload suggestions and choose mode per preference.
  • Reset coach intro/info dialogs if you previously selected "Don’t show again".

Privacy

GymTracker is designed to respect your privacy. You can log and review your training without creating an account or sending workout data to a server.

What the app does (and doesn’t) collect

No account required No workout data sent to a server All logs stored locally on device

Local training data Workout days, exercises, completed sessions, sets (weight & reps), warm‑up notes, coach notes. Stored on your device to keep your training history and allow the coach to make suggestions.
App settings Theme choice, warm‑up & rest timer settings, coach modes (progression/deload/auto‑apply). Stored locally so the app remembers your preferences.

Support & contact

If you encounter issues, have questions about how the coach works or want to suggest features, you can contact the developer directly.

Is GymTracker free?

The base version of GymTracker is free to download from the Google Play Store. Some future versions may offer optional paid features, but core workout logging and coach functionality are planned to stay available without a subscription.

Do I need an account or internet connection?

No account is required to use GymTracker. All workout logs and settings are stored locally on your device. An internet connection is only needed to download the app, receive updates and to send anonymous crash reports if you have that enabled via Google Play.

Which devices are supported?

GymTracker is designed for Android phones and tablets running Android 8.0 or newer. On the Play Store, you can see compatibility information for your own devices when you are logged in with your Google account.
